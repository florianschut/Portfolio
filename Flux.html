Flux - A Global Game Jam Game

A Physics based party game made in 48 Hours for Global Game Jam 2018

  • Role: Gameplay Programmer
  • Project Length: 48 Hours
  • Period: January 26-28 2018
  • Team members: 18
  • Responsibilities
    • Character movement
    • Generalist tasks

Flux is 2-4 player local co-op party game in which you play as a transcendent crystal being with the power to telepathically move objects around you. The player has to use his telepathic powers to lift and throw objects in the arena to the other players and be the last man standing. The player has the ability to stop any incoming threats by timing his powers, giving the objects extra speed on his next throw. This project was made in 48 hours for the 2018 Global Game Jam.

My Contribution

Flux was my first experience using Git for source control and also almost my first experience with C++ in Unreal Engine 4 thus leading to a steep learning curve. Combine this with having 48 hours to create a game and we had an unforgettable experience. Back to the contributions, I started the weekend working on the player movement, making it feel just right since our game was heavily focused on moving around and manipulating physics. After this time was spend adding art assets like particle effects to many different objects, creating a UI although I was not in charge of creating the assets for this. Next followed a load of bug fixes and the inevitable blurry section of the game jam. I proceeded to add a shield mechanic to the player and many art assets made by our huge amount of artists. Despite only having 48 hours it might have been the most interesting project I've done in Unreal filled with learning, self-development and lasting memories.
