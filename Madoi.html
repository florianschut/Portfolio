Ma ĐÓI - A Custom Engine Powered Game

A 2D sidescrolling platformer made for PSVita and Windows 10 using a custom made engine: IGArt

  • Role: PSVita Graphics Programmer
  • Project Length: 16 Weeks
  • Period: February 2018 - July 2018
  • Team members: 17
  • Responsibilities
    • PlayStation Vita rendering

MA ĐÓI is a 2D Auto Runner Action Platformer in which the player is given the ability of gravity manipulation on a global scale making certain objects float up and sink down. All while the player jumps and punches through various obstacles and explores the multiple paths of the levels leading them towards the end goal.

My Contribution

I started Ma ĐÓI with a very shameful track record in terms of graphics programming. This only served to make me more eager to tackle the challenge head-on and be solely responsible for the rendering on the PlayStation Vita. The initial weeks were spend both understanding the Vita's rendering API and learning a lot of graphics concepts in the process. The first triangles on this exotic device I barely saw before felt like a triumph and spurred me on to continue working with unrelenting motivation. To everyone's surprise, including mine, we had our demo running on the Vita during our presentation at the halfway point before it would be decided if our engine team would go on to create a game the next 8 weeks. Being able to continue this project drastically increased my excitement and confidence regarding graphics programming. The remaining features were all added on the same level of quality as our DirectX 12 renderer made by teammates of mine. Even though I'm aware of the complexity of DirectX 12 compared to the more straightforward Vita rendering, it still gives me a spark of pride that the Vita ended up running with lower frame times than the DirectX 12 renderer on our dedicated graphics cards.
